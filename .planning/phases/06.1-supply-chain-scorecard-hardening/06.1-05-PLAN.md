<pitfalls>
- **Pitfall 1 — Grouping security updates by accident:** Dependabot has both `version-updates` and `security-updates` PR types. Security updates SHOULD stay individual (faster turnaround on vulnerabilities). `applies-to: version-updates` makes this explicit; do NOT change to `applies-to: all`.
- **Pitfall 2 — Major bumps in the group:** Major-version bumps are breaking by definition. Grouping them with minor/patch hides the breaking diff. Keep `update-types: [minor, patch]` exactly — never add `major`.
- **Pitfall 3 — Group name change breaking PR history:** Once Dependabot opens PRs under a group name, changing the name later orphans the in-flight PRs (Dependabot considers them stale and may close + reopen). Pick `actions-minor-patch` and stick with it.
- **Pitfall 4 — `open-pull-requests-limit: 5` still applies WITHIN a group:** Grouping reduces PR count but does not lift the limit. With grouping, 5 PRs is plenty (typically 1 group + a few major bumps).
- **Pitfall 5 — Manual `# vX.Y.Z` comment edits:** PATTERNS §3 reminder. After this plan ships, maintainers MUST let Dependabot manage the comment text in lockstep with SHA. The added top-of-file warning surfaces this to future readers.
</pitfalls>
